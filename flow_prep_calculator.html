Staining volume
The staining volume is how much liquid is in each tube while your cells incubate with the antibodies. Standard is 100 µL — datasheets are calibrated to this. Your cells get resuspended directly into this volume (buffer + antibodies already mixed together). The number on the right is how much total cocktail to prepare.

Washing
After incubation, you wash cells to remove unbound antibody. Add a large volume of the same PBS or FACS buffer you always use — nothing special — centrifuge, aspirate. This removes background signal. For surface staining, 1 wash is standard.

Why dilute?
Most datasheets say 1–2 µL per sample. Pipetting 1 µL accurately is really hard — a small wobble is a 20% error. The fix: dilute the antibody first into a fresh tube, then pipette a larger, more manageable volume that delivers the exact same amount of antibody.

If an antibody already calls for ≥ your target volume per sample, no dilution is needed — pull straight from the vial.
Target pipetting volume (µL)
What you want to pipette per sample. 5 µL is accurate and manageable.
Dead volume per Ab (µL)
Extra to prepare per antibody to cover tip losses. 20 µL is safe.

FMO tubes
An FMO tube contains all antibodies except the one you are gating on. It shows where the true negative sits for that channel when all other fluorochromes are present (which spill into each other). Treat every FMO tube identically to your samples — same incubation, same wash, same DAPI.
